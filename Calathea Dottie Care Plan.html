Calathea Dottie Care Plan

Rose-Painted Prayer Plant Care Guide

Lighting

Environment & Sunlight

Native to the Brazilian rainforest understory, the Calathea Dottie thrives in conditions that mimic a shaded forest floor.

  • Ideal Light: Medium to bright, indirect light is best.
  • Avoid Direct Sun: Harsh rays can bleach or scorch the deep green and pink foliage.
  • Low Light Tolerance: It can tolerate lower light levels, but growth will slow and colors may fade.
Pro Tip: Rotate your plant weekly to ensure even growth and prevent it from leaning toward the light source.
Hydration

Watering & Soil

Calatheas are notoriously "fussy" about their water quality and frequency.

  • Moisture Level: Keep the soil consistently moist but never soggy. Water when the top inch of soil feels dry.
  • Water Quality: Highly sensitive to fluoride and chemicals in tap water. Use distilled, filtered, or rainwater to prevent brown edges.
  • Soil Mix: Use a well-draining, slightly acidic potting medium. A mix of potting soil, perlite, orchid bark, and peat is ideal.
Bottom Watering: Placing the pot in a shallow dish of water for 15-20 minutes can help keep soil evenly moist without over-saturating the top layer.
Climate

Temperature & Humidity

Humidity is non-negotiable for Calathea Dottie health.

  • Humidity Level: Minimum 50-60%, ideally 70%. If air is too dry, leaf edges will turn crispy and brown.
  • Temperature: Prefers 65°F to 85°F (18°C-30°C). Do not let temperatures drop below 60°F.
  • Draft Warning: Keep away from AC vents, heaters, and cold window drafts.
Humidity Hacks: Group plants together, use a pebble tray, or place a humidifier within 3 feet of the plant for best results.
Troubleshooting

Common Problems & Fixes

Symptom Likely Cause Quick Fix
Curling Leaves Underwatering / Low Humidity Water immediately and boost humidity.
Crispy Brown Edges Hard Water / Dry Air Switch to distilled water; use a humidifier.
Yellowing Leaves Overwatering / Poor Drainage Check for root rot; let soil dry out more.
Faded Foliage Too Much Direct Sun Move to a more shaded location.
Ongoing Care

Feeding & Maintenance

  • Fertilizer: Feed monthly during spring and summer with a balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half-strength.
  • Pruning: Snip off yellow or damaged leaves at the base with sterilized shears to encourage new growth.
  • Cleaning: Wipe leaves with a damp cloth once a month to remove dust and help the plant photosynthesize effectively.
  • Pests: Watch for spider mites and mealybugs. Treat with neem oil or insecticidal soap if spotted.
